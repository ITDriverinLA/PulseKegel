PulseKegel – Onboarding Flow Requirements
Version: 1.0
Goal: Quick, respectful, gender-aware onboarding that gets users into the 7-day challenge as fast as possible while making them feel understood.
1. Screen 1 – Welcome / Gender Selection
Headline (large, centered):
Most people lose strength in their pelvic floor over time.
You can train it — and you should.
Sub-headline (smaller):
Let’s get started.
Two large buttons (side by side or stacked):

I’m a Man
I’m a Woman

Flow:
User taps one → immediately goes to Screen 2.

2. Screen 2 – Kegel Knowledge Check
Headline:
Do you know how to do Kegels?
Two buttons:

Yes, I know how
No, show me how

Flow:

If Yes → go directly to Screen 4 (Final CTA)
If No → go to Screen 3 (Tutorial)


3. Screen 3 – Tutorial (only shown if “No” was chosen)
Two-step animation / illustration (simple, clean, gender-specific)
For Men:

Step 1 – Find the muscle
Squeeze the same muscle you use to stop your pee mid-flow.
(Highlight the pelvic floor muscle on a simple male silhouette)
Step 2 – Activate it
Now lift your balls upward using just that muscle.
That’s a Kegel. No one can see you doing it.

For Women:

Step 1 – Find the muscle
Gently squeeze the same muscles you use to stop the flow of urine.
Step 2 – Activate it
Now slowly draw that area upward and inward — like you’re lifting a marble inside you.
That’s a Kegel.

Button at bottom:
Got it – Let’s begin
Flow: After tapping “Got it” → go to Screen 4.

4. Screen 4 – Final Call-to-Action (shown for both paths)
Headline (gender-specific):
For Men:
Your pelvic floor affects your bladder control, confidence, and performance.
Most men never train it.
For Women:
Your pelvic floor affects bladder control, core strength, and intimacy.
Most women only start training it after they notice a problem.
Big primary button (centered, high contrast):
Start My 7-Day Challenge
Secondary text (small, below button):
Takes less than 5 minutes a day. Your data stays private.

Additional Notes for Developer:

Keep the entire flow to maximum 4 screens (including tutorial).
Use large, tappable buttons and generous spacing.
Make the gender-specific headlines and tutorial text feel supportive, not clinical.
After “Start My 7-Day Challenge” → drop user straight into the first day of the challenge (no extra friction).
