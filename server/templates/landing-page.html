Frequently Asked Questions

What are kegel exercises and why should I do them?

Kegel exercises strengthen the pelvic floor muscles that support your bladder, bowel, and sexual function. Regular kegel training can improve bladder control, reduce incontinence, and enhance sexual health. PulseKegel guides you through a structured 12-week program with 7 different exercise types for comprehensive pelvic floor strengthening.

How long does each PulseKegel workout take?

PulseKegel workouts take just 5-10 minutes per day. The app's 12-week program starts with shorter sessions in the Control Phase and gradually builds to more comprehensive workouts in the Power Phase, all designed to fit into a busy schedule.

How does the haptic feedback work during exercises?

PulseKegel uses your phone's vibration motor to provide distinct haptic patterns for each exercise type. Slow holds use steady pulses with progressive intensity, quick flicks use rapid staccato bursts, and elevator exercises use stepped patterns. The intensity matches the visual power bar so you can feel the rhythm without looking at the screen.

Is PulseKegel suitable for men?

Yes, PulseKegel is designed for anyone who wants to strengthen their pelvic floor. Pelvic floor exercises are beneficial for men of all ages and can help with bladder control, post-prostate surgery recovery, and overall pelvic health.

Is my health data private and secure?
